Andhra-Style Pulihora (Tamarind Rice)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (cooled)
  • 1 lemon-sized ball of tamarind (soaked in warm water)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil (preferred)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1 tsp urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 dry red chili, broken
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • Few curry leaves
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1–2 tbsp roasted peanuts or cashews
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Procedure

  1. Extract tamarind pulp by squeezing the soaked tamarind and straining it.
  2. Boil the pulp in a pan until thick and raw smell disappears (about 8–10 minutes). Add turmeric and salt. Let it cool.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
  4. Add chana dal, urad dal, red chili, green chili, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Sauté until golden.
  5. Add the thick tamarind paste and cook for another 2–3 minutes with the tempering.
  6. Mix this entire spiced tamarind mixture with the cooled rice. Adjust salt.
  7. Top with roasted peanuts or cashews and gently mix.
  8. Let it rest for 15–20 minutes before serving to allow flavors to infuse.
