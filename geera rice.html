Fragrant Jeera Rice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1¾ cups water
  • 1½ tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • 1–2 cloves (optional)
  • ½ inch cinnamon stick (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish)

Procedure

  1. Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak for 20–30 minutes. Drain before cooking.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add whole spices (if using) and sauté for a few seconds for aroma.
  4. Add soaked rice and gently stir for 1–2 minutes to coat in ghee.
  5. Pour in measured water and add salt to taste. Stir once and bring to a boil.
  6. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and cook for 12–15 minutes without lifting the lid.
  7. Turn off heat and let it sit covered for another 5 minutes.
  8. Fluff gently with a fork and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  9. Serve warm with dal, curry, or raita.
