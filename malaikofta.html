Rich & Creamy Malai Kofta

Ingredients

For the Kofta

  • 2 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • ½ cup paneer (grated)
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Chopped cashews and raisins (for stuffing)
  • Oil for deep frying

For the Gravy

  • 2 tbsp oil or butter
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp cashew paste or 10 cashews soaked & blended
  • ¼ cup cream or milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Chopped coriander for garnish

Procedure

Making the Koftas

  1. Mix mashed potatoes, paneer, cornflour, salt, pepper, and garam masala to form a dough.
  2. Make small balls, stuff each with a piece of cashew and raisin in the center.
  3. Heat oil and deep fry koftas until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.

Preparing the Gravy

  1. Heat oil or butter in a pan. Sauté onions until translucent.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell is gone.
  3. Add tomato puree and cook until oil starts to separate.
  4. Stir in red chili, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt.
  5. Add cashew paste and cook for 2–3 minutes, then add cream or milk.
  6. Add a little water for desired consistency and let it simmer.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala, stir, and turn off the heat.

To Serve

  1. Place koftas in the serving bowl and pour hot gravy over them.
  2. Garnish with coriander and a drizzle of cream. Serve with naan, roti, or jeera rice!
