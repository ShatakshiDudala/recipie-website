Traditional Mutton Curry

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (bone-in, cleaned)
  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 onions (finely sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped or pureed)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ cup thick curd (whisked)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1½ tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water as needed for gravy

Procedure

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices and let them crackle.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Mix in ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and oil separates.
  5. Add turmeric, chili, coriander, and cumin powders. Stir well.
  6. Add mutton pieces. Sauté for 6–8 minutes until lightly browned and coated in masala.
  7. Lower heat, add whisked curd gradually, stirring continuously to avoid curdling.
  8. Pour water to cover the meat. Add salt and cover the pot.
  9. Cook on low heat for 45–60 minutes until meat is tender (or use a pressure cooker for faster results).
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with rice or naan!
